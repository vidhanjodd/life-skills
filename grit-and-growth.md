# Grit and Growth Mindset

## 1. Grit

Grit is the drive and tenacity to pursue an objective over time, even when it gets difficult or tedious. Working hard is more important for success than having innate talent.


## 2. Overview of the Growth Mindset


The belief that intelligence and skills can be enhanced through practice, education, and hard work is known as a growth mindset. Failure and obstacles are chances for development rather than signs of limitations.


## 3. Internal Control Center

### Internal Locus of Control: What is it?

The notion that your success or failure is mostly determined by your own choices, actions, and efforts rather than by chance or outside factors is known as internal locus of control.

### Key Point from the Video:

The overriding message is that assuming responsibility for your outcomes will improve your motivation and development. By concentrating on what you can control, you will be more productive and better able to withstand adversity.


## 4. Crucial Elements to Develop a Growth Mentality

- Have faith that abilities can be acquired
- Accept challenges.Take constructive criticism to heart.
- Continue in spite of setbacks.
- "I can learn" should be used instead of "I can't."
- Prioritize effort over skill.


## 5. My Action Plan for Software Developers to Develop a Growth Mindset

- Every day, practice coding, even if it's just a little bit.
- Instead of avoiding difficult problems, find solutions to them.
- Consider errors and bugs as teaching moments.
- Seek mentors' input and make improvements in response to it.
- Monitor your weekly progress in your coding abilities.
- Consistently learn new technologies
